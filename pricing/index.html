Market exclusivity.
Built in, not an upsell.

Operators rightly worry: if you build this for me, what stops you from selling the same cockpit to my direct competitor across town? Two answers.

First, the technical reality: every cockpit is custom-fit to your accounts, your workflow, your team structure, your branding. Even if a direct competitor hired us for the same vertical, what we'd build them would be different — built around their accounts, their workflow.

Second, the contractual answer: every build includes a market-exclusivity clause by default.

The exclusivity guarantee Included

For 24 months from launch, within 25 miles of your primary business address, we will not build a cockpit in your industry for another operator. Period. If a competitor inquires, we tell them the territory is taken and refer them to a different vertical or location.

The cockpit runs on real cloud infrastructure (typically Supabase + Cloudflare or Deno Deploy in US regions). Those services bill at consumption — usually $30 to $60 per month for a typical operator-sized cockpit.

Pricing FAQ.

Is the $3,500–$8,500 monthly or one-time?

One-time. You pay once when the cockpit ships, and the cockpit is yours. There is no recurring "Heisenbug subscription" you pay forever. The only recurring cost is the actual cloud hosting (~$30–60/month), which you can pay your cloud provider directly if you prefer.

Why is the range so wide ($3,500 to $8,500)?

Operators come in different shapes. A solo HVAC contractor with one truck and one phone number needs less than a 12-location home health agency with state EVV compliance and three integrations. We size your build at the kickoff call after the discovery — by the time you get the number, you'll understand exactly why it lands where it does.

What does market exclusivity actually mean?

For 24 months from the day your cockpit goes live, within 25 miles of your primary business address, we will not build a cockpit in your industry for another operator. If a direct competitor reaches out, we tell them the territory is taken and either decline or steer them to a different vertical / city. You can extend the radius to 50 miles ($1,500 one time) or statewide ($4,000 one time). National exclusivity by request.

What if I want to keep evolving the cockpit after the first version?

Three options. (1) Take the code and run. You own it — bring in your own developer or do it yourself. (2) Pay for add-ons à la carte. Each new panel, integration, or feature gets a flat-fee quote upfront — see the add-on menu above. (3) Opt into the $200/month priority response retainer. 24-hour response on issues, ad-hoc work billed at flat fee. Most operators stay on option 1 or 2.

Can I pay in installments?

Yes. Typical structure is 40% at kickoff (after discovery, before we start coding), 40% at the first working version (day 7–10), 20% at the end of the refinement window. We can adjust the structure for cash-flow-sensitive operators — say so at the kickoff call.

What if I want a refund?

If the first working version doesn't fit your business at all — not "not perfect," but "not the shape of my workflow at all" — we refund the kickoff payment and the cockpit code stays with you. We've never had this happen because we discover hard before we build. But it's the honest answer to a fair question.

Do you do equity deals or revenue share instead of cash?

No. We're not investors and we're not a startup studio. The flat-fee model is simple by design — for both sides. Operators run on cash; we want cash; equity gets messy.

What about non-profits, government, or operators in severe cash-flow distress?

We've done sliding-scale work in specific cases (typically family connections or operators whose existing software is actively bankrupting them). Ask directly. The answer is usually yes, with a clear conversation about why and how much.

What does the kickoff call actually look like?

30 minutes on the phone or video. We ask three things: (1) walk us through how you actually run a day in your business, what the tabs and tools are; (2) what hurts the most that we should fix first; (3) what's your budget reality. We listen mostly. By the end of the call you either have a number and a delivery date, or you have an honest "we're not the right fit for this." No follow-up sales calls. No proposals you'll wait two weeks for.

Ready for a number?

The fastest way to know what your cockpit would cost is the 30-minute discovery call. We'll know enough by the end of it to give you a flat fee and a delivery date.
