HVAC service inventory · San Antonio

Try the demo →

→ The operator

An independent HVAC service company in San Antonio, TX. 4 service trucks, 3 lead techs plus a foreman, residential + light-commercial work, ~$1.2M in annual revenue. Has been running on a shared Google Sheet, a parts envelope in the foreman's truck, and a group text for 8 years.

→ What hurt

The foreman estimated they were eating ~$48,000 a year in parts. Couldn't tell you which truck had which condenser fan, which compressor, which capacitor. Techs would drive 45 min back to the warehouse for a part they had on a different truck. Refrigerant cylinders had unknown owners and unknown weights — EPA Section 608 logbook was a paper notebook updated maybe weekly. Insurance audit was approaching with no reliable refrigerant log.

→ What we built

~10 days
From first call to live in trucks
3 panels
Truck inventory · Refrigerant log · Owner view

A phone-first PWA installed to each truck's iPhone. Barcode scan with the phone camera reads parts in/out. Voice query — "where's the two-ton condenser fan motor" — returns truck location + bin number, instantly. Refrigerant cylinder weighing screen logs in/out with EPA Section 608 compliance, auto-generates the annual log report. Owner view aggregates parts by truck, surfaces low-stock, flags any cylinder that hasn't been weighed in 30 days.

→ What changed in 90 days

~$11k
Estimated parts shrinkage prevented quarter 1
2.3 hrs/wk
Drive time saved per tech vs the spreadsheet era
What it replacedGoogle Sheets · paper refrigerant logbook · group text for parts requests · a 2010-era handheld scanner the foreman stopped using two years ago

Vending + coffee route ops · Pennsylvania

Try the demo →

→ The operator

A multi-state vending and coffee-route operator headquartered in Pennsylvania. ~80 placed machines, multiple states, mix of cantaloupe NayaX telemetry + DEX/MDB hardware + cash-only legacy units. Solo operator + a route helper.

→ What hurt

The operator drove each route weekly whether the machines needed service or not. No telemetry visibility — couldn't tell from home which machines were empty, which were faulted, which had a coin jam. Couldn't tell which placements were profitable. Tax time was a shoebox of receipts and a guess.

→ What we built

~14 days
First version live on phone + iPad
6 modules
Route · Telemetry · Cashless · Location scout · Per-machine P&L · Receipt capture

Today's route built dynamically from telemetry — only machines that need service, ordered by drive-time optimization. Cashless integration with NayaX + Cantaloupe APIs to know exactly how much each machine cleared this week. Location-scout engine that surfaces new placement opportunities from local business permits + openings. Per-machine P&L showing which placements actually pay rent and which don't. Receipt capture during pickup — photograph any commission receipt, it auto-files for tax.

→ What changed in the first quarter

~40%
Reduction in drive miles per week
7 machines
Identified as unprofitable, repositioned or retired

The operator runs it under the brand BrewRoute (the cockpit ships behind a per-operator brand identity). Live at vending-route.com.

What it replacedHand-drawn route map · shoebox of vendor receipts · two separate dashboards (NayaX + Cantaloupe) opened in browser tabs · weekly drive of all machines whether they needed it or not

Home health care agency · Texas

Try the demo →

→ The operator

A non-medical home health agency in Texas serving ~50 active patients with a roster of 12-14 caregivers. Mix of Medicaid waiver (CDPAP-style), Medicare home health, LTC insurance, and private pay. State EVV mandate fully applies.

→ What hurt

The agency administrator was running on seven disconnected tools: scheduler in Calendly, EVV in the state's vendor app, charting in Google Docs, payroll in QuickBooks, family messaging via text, compliance tracking in a spreadsheet, billing in HCHB. Every shift change meant updating the same patient's status in four places. Caregivers used two apps on their phone for one visit. Family members called the office to ask if mom got her medication. Every state inspection meant a week of preparation.

→ What we built

~18 days
First version live · two-week iteration after
6 panels + caregiver phone
Schedule · EVV · Care plans · Family · Compliance · Billing · Caregiver app

One cockpit for the office. One phone app for the caregivers. Shift assignment drives EVV, EVV drives the visit log, the visit log drives the billing batch, the billing batch drives the payroll calc. No double entry. Family members get a sanitized post-visit summary on their phone within 15 minutes of the caregiver checking out. RN supervisor and family on one HIPAA-aligned message thread per patient. Compliance dashboard auto-blocks any caregiver with a lapsed CPR / TB / background check before they can be assigned a visit.

→ What changed in the first 90 days

~14 hrs/mo
Admin time saved by replacing 7 tools with 1
98%+
EVV compliance vs prior 89% (drives Medicaid payment reliability)
"It's the only place in my day where everything is in one place. State inspector this month asked me three questions, I answered three from one screen, she was out in twenty minutes."
— Agency administrator (anonymized at her request)
What it replacedCalendly · the state EVV vendor app · Google Docs for charts · QuickBooks for payroll · group texts for family · spreadsheet for compliance · HCHB for billing
