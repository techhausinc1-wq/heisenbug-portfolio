→ A free hub for U.S. veterans

Straight lines to real help.

VetSource points a U.S. veteran to the right phone number on the first try instead of the fifteenth.

Crisis support, housing, benefits, records, local programs. Plain language. No login. No paperwork. No data collection. Free for vets, forever.

→ The why

One civilian who owes this country something.

VetSource is built and run by Ivan Svedov, founder of Heisenbug, with domain expertise from a Marine who knows the system from the inside. Neither of us are vets. The site exists because the path from "I need help" to actually getting it shouldn't take twelve hours of phone tag, a half-broken government portal, and a war story for every gatekeeper.

The right phone number, the right form, the right local program — on the first try. That's the whole product. No SaaS, no upsell, no subscription, no premium tier. The build is funded out of pocket. It will stay that way.

→ What's in it

Nine cabinets. The things that matter on the worst day.

Each cabinet collapses a category of help into one page: the phone number that actually gets a human, the form that actually moves, the local door that actually opens.

Crisis & mental health

988 press 1 (Veterans Crisis Line), text 838255, chat. Local crisis options. No-cost counseling routes. What to do in the next thirty minutes.

Housing & homelessness

HUD-VASH vouchers, SSVF rapid re-housing, GPD transitional housing, National Call Center for Homeless Veterans (877-424-3838). Tonight, this week, this month.

Benefits & claims

VA disability claims, appeals, the difference between PACT Act and pre-PACT filings. Free VSO (Veterans Service Officer) lookup — you don't pay a lawyer for this.

ID & records

DD-214 replacement, VA ID card, state veteran ID. eBenefits, Vets.gov, the National Personnel Records Center. The exact link, not three layers of menus.

Healthcare

VA enrollment, community care eligibility, the local VA medical center. Toxic exposure registry. What's free, what's not, what the wait actually is.

Jobs & transition

Vocational Rehabilitation (Chapter 31), VA Work-Study, federal hiring preference, veteran-owned business certification (VOSB / SDVOSB). The doors that exist that you weren't told about.

Education & GI Bill

Post-9/11 GI Bill, transfer to dependents, Yellow Ribbon, VR&E. The schools that actually honor it. The schools that don't.

Women, MST, family

Women veteran call center (855-829-6636), Military Sexual Trauma counselors, family caregiver support, survivor benefits. Specific lines, specific advocates.

The community

A future peer space where vets talk to vets, reviewed by a real person, no algorithm. Currently a waitlist. Will not ship until it can ship safely.

→ What this isn't, and never will be

The pledge.

Free, forever. No exceptions.

  • No login, no account, no email capture as the price of entry.
  • No tracking. No Google Analytics, no Facebook pixel, no third-party scripts following anyone around the internet.
  • No data sold. Nothing typed into VetSource is stored. Nothing is shared.
  • No paid tier. There is no "premium" version. There never will be.
  • No affiliation with the VA. VetSource is independent. It just points to the right doors.
  • No AI hallucinations dressed up as advice. Every phone number, link, and program description is checked against a primary source.
→ Why it's on heisenbug.app

One cockpit pattern, applied to a different problem.

VetSource is built on the same toolkit as every other Heisenbug cockpit — single-file frontend, fast on a five-year-old phone, offline-capable, accessible, no dependency on a server we might shut off in three years. The catalog charges for that toolkit. This one doesn't. Same engine, different posture.

If you run an operation that helps veterans — a county VSO, a 501(c)(3), a transitional housing org — and there's something missing or wrong on VetSource, tell us. The fix usually ships the same day.
